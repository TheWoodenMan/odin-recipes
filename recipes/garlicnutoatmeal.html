This receipe was adapted from a traditional family scottish recipe taught to me by my parents

Skirlie is normally served as a side dish, but I needed some actual stuffing to go with a roast and decided to experiment

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup medium pinhead oatmeal
  • 1 medium onion
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • 1 cup chopped cashew nuts
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • 1 tsp sage/li>
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 3-4 cloves of garlic

The first time I made this it caused the chicken I stuffed it with to be a little undercooked requiring more time in the oven. Just bear that in mind, it's quite dense. Cooking it separately in an oven tray is better.

Method:

  1. Saute onions & garlic in oil on a medium heat until translucent
  2. Add oatmeal to the mix a tablespoonful at a time, stirring and heating through
  3. add cashew nuts mixing through
  4. add in the green herbs and stir until mixed in
  5. bake for 25-30 minutes at 180 degrees C or gas mark 5
  6. remove from the oven when it's just starting to brown

Key Point: oatmeal burns really easily, you will need to stir this constantly until off the heat

Key Point: Chop the cashew nuts in a blender if you like but don't go too small, you want a little crunch.

Wine Suggestions

This is a stuffing so any wine that goes with roast dinner will work, lighter, jammy reds would complement the herbacious tastes or a buttery oaked white.

  • Montepulciano de Abruzzo
  • Californian Pinot Noir
  • Adelaide Hills, Australian Chardonnay
  • Southern French, Languedoc Chardonnay
